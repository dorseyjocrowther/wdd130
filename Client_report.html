Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I chose the content and images for my site plan based on the goal for the site and the intended audience. As my site is meant to inform my wedding guests of the details of the ceremony and reception, while also allowing them to RSVP, and access the wedding registry, I wanted the site to be as easy to navigate as possible while still being stylized to reflect the theme of the event.This objective guided every decision I made regarding the site content.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my lead developer via Microsoft Teams. There were no issues with communication as she was quick to reply and reached out with updates on the site weekly.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

My developer did meet my expectations for my site. I was surprised at how well my developer was able to match the wireframe I provided as the pages were quite heavily stylized with floral embelishments.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

To make the process smoother as a client I would work on the wireframe for my site longer. This would have allowed me to provide more content for the site, such as links to the wedding registry and more details on the venue. While I am extremely happy with the result of my site, more time to carefully craft a wireframe would have resulted in a complete, ready-to-use site.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

I communicated with my team leads via groupchat in Microsoft teams. Communication with the leads was slow which resulted in most of my work as a junior developer being done independently in order to meet due dates.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

The leads I worked with did well with assigning child pages early on and shared the client wireframes with the team, so I did know what was expected for each site.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

I was able to contribute to my sites. The home pages for the sites are either not completed or not created at all which made linking my work to the home page difficult. However, all of the html and css for my child pages are complete.

I did run into some issues organizing my work with the other junior developers work in GitHub as we were both trying to make our childpages independently. These issues were easy to navigate as it only required some deletion and reuploading of code to organize pages into folders.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part of this project for me was navigating the schedules of other members of my group. Being an online class, many of the group member had demanding schedules. This made getting work done as a group difficult especially as a Junior developer as each week I would wait until Saturday for any uploads or contact from the lead. Ultimately every assignment as a junior developer had to be done independently rather than coordinating with the lead developers. This was difficult to navigate as I did not want to nag the leads but also wanted to do the assignment as correctly as possible. Ultimately I so feel I was able to overcome this challenge by doing my best to get my portions of the project done on time.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed watching the wireframe of my clients come together as they had envisioned. Throughout this project I would hope that the changes I wanted to apply were going to work as I wrote out code. Getting to test the link and see my work as I had hoped was always very satisfying.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do the project again I would start by linking my child pages to the home page. I tried linking my work as a junior developer at the end and it was stressful trying to make sure the links worked correctly. Linking my page first and then writing the code after would have made the project less stressful.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

This experience did increase my confidence in working on real-world projects. Initially I was doubtful that I could pull off making the site match the clients wireframes well, but it ended up being much easier than I anticipated. Breaking down the project week-by-week and focusing on one aspect each week (html and css) made the project much less intimidating as everything came together piece by piece.
